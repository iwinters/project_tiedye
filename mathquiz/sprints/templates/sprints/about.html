I hated math as a kid. I did everything you’re supposed to: I did my homework, took extra math lessons outside of school, had it explained to me over and over again. I got alright grades, but only because I could memorize the answers - it never actually clicked.

In grade 10, questions became more complex, and memorizing answers didn’t work anymore. I ran into a wall, and my grades slipped in a major way. So I started avoiding math altogether. Beyond that though, I started believing that I just wasn’t good at that type of thing.

It wasn’t until I was in my 20s and was teaching myself to develop software, that I realized that math and logic problems could be fun, and satisfying - but you need to get them, from the ground up.

Math, science and engineering are intimidating because of the way we teach them. If we want our kids to become the next generation of great engineers, architects, and astronauts, we have to help them to be great at math, not just memorize the answers.
